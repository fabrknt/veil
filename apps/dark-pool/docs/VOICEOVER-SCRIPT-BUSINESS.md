# Veil Dark Pool — Business Pitch Voiceover Script

> 12 slides, ~3 minutes. This is the pitch video for judges.
> Record yourself on Loom (face + screen) scrolling through business.html.
> Speak as a founder pitching to a VC, not as an engineer explaining code.

---

## [Slide 1] Title — 10 seconds

"Veil Dark Pool. The on-chain equivalent of what NYSE already operates — built for Solana."

---

## [Slide 2] Why Now — 15 seconds

"Seven trillion dollars a month trades through perp markets. In TradFi, thirty to fifty percent of equity volume goes through dark pools. On Solana — the number is zero. Every large trade gets front-run, sandwiched, or copied. Institutional capital won't enter until execution is private."

---

## [Slide 3] Competitive Landscape — 15 seconds

"No one occupies this position. Flashbots and CoW Protocol protect spot trades. Jito protects at the validator level. Umbra hides wallet identity. Silhouette built a dark pool, but only for Hyperliquid. Application-layer privacy for perps on Solana — nobody is here. That's where we sit."

---

## [Slide 4] Confidentiality vs Anonymity — 15 seconds

"This is not Tornado Cash. Tornado Cash hides who you are — permanently, no audit trail. That's why it was sanctioned. Veil hides what you trade — temporarily, with a full audit trail on-chain. Dark pools are legal. NYSE, NASDAQ, every major broker operates them under SEC regulation. We're bringing a proven, regulated model on-chain."

---

## [Slide 5] Why Solana — 12 seconds

"Why not build on Hyperliquid like Silhouette? Because they're locked to one venue with zero composability. On Solana, Veil settles across Drift, Jupiter Perps, and Phoenix in atomic transactions. Any protocol can CPI into the dark pool. That's a moat Hyperliquid structurally cannot match."

---

## [Slide 6] Go-to-Market — 18 seconds

"We start with ourselves. I run Yogi on Drift mainnet and Kodiak on Hyperliquid. Every rebalance leaks intent and loses money to MEV. We're our own first customer."

"Phase two: other vault operators. Liminal, Harmonix, Reflect — they all have the same problem. Integration is one function call."

"Phase three: institutional desks. Dark pools are table stakes for their flow. Phase four: protocol-level CPI integration — distribution through composability."

---

## [Slide 7] Revenue — 12 seconds

"Revenue scales with volume, not capital. Two to five basis points per matched trade. Same fee model as IEX — three hundred million dollars annual revenue. Same model as Liquidnet — acquired for seven hundred million. This business model is proven. We're bringing it on-chain."

---

## [Slide 8] Traction — 12 seconds

"We're not starting from zero. Eighty tests passing. Program deployed on devnet. End-to-end lifecycle working — encrypt, commit, match, settle. Three settlement venues wired. And this is built on four months of production infrastructure — npm packages, live vaults on Drift and Hyperliquid mainnet."

---

## [Slide 9] Team — 15 seconds

"I'm a solo founder based in Tokyo. I run live perp vaults — Yogi on Drift, Kodiak on Hyperliquid. I built Veil's privacy infrastructure — six apps, published on npm. I'm not building a dark pool because it sounds interesting. I'm building it because I lose money to MEV on every rebalance. I need this product myself."

---

## [Slide 10] The Pivot — 15 seconds

"I started this hackathon building Syntx — a cross-venue perp DEX. Five venue adapters, fifteen instructions, full frontend. Mentors told me the truth: you're competing with Drift and Jupiter, and you have no edge."

"But they loved the privacy angle. So I pivoted. Took the Veil encryption stack, added a matching engine, and built the first dark pool for Solana perps. In one week. Eighty tests. Deployed on devnet."

---

## [Slide 11] The Ask — 12 seconds

"What we need: a security audit to unlock mainnet, and three months to integrate TEE with QuickNode Streams for real-time matching. What we bring: production privacy infrastructure, live mainnet vaults, a working dark pool on devnet, and our own vaults as the first customer. This is a novel primitive — the first of its kind on Solana."

---

## [Slide 12] Close — 10 seconds

*(Slow. Let it land.)*

"Dark pools handle half of institutional equity volume for a reason. Solana perps deserve the same infrastructure."

*(Pause.)*

"Veil Dark Pool. First of its kind. Built on production systems. Ready for mainnet."

## Recording Tips

1. **Use Loom** — face + screen. Judges back founders they can picture. Your face at the top corner while scrolling through the deck is more powerful than slides alone.

2. **Total: ~3 minutes** — allocate roughly 10-18 seconds per slide. If you're running long, cut the Go-to-Market to just Phase 1 and 2.

3. **Energy curve:**
   - Slides 1-3: Confident, setting the stage
   - Slide 4 (Tornado Cash): Firm, authoritative — this is the legal distinction
   - Slides 6-7 (GTM + Revenue): Business-like, specific numbers
   - Slide 9 (Team): Personal, authentic — "I need this myself"
   - Slide 10 (Pivot): Honest, shows self-awareness
   - Slide 12 (Close): Slow, deliberate, memorable

4. **Key phrases to nail:**
   - "The on-chain equivalent of what NYSE already operates"
   - "This is not Tornado Cash"
   - "I lose money to MEV on every rebalance"
   - "First of its kind"

5. **Don't memorize** — read it a few times, then speak naturally. The script is a guide. Conviction matters more than polish.

6. **If you only have 2 minutes:** Cut slides 3 (landscape), 5 (why Solana), and 6 (GTM details). Keep: problem → Tornado Cash distinction → revenue → traction → team → pivot → ask → close.
